# Ephemeris audit — astronomy-engine vs Swiss Ephemeris

Differential audit of every aṅga boundary and every festival-date decision, comparing the engine's ephemeris ([astronomy-engine](https://github.com/cosinekitty/astronomy) v2.1.19, stated accuracy ±1′) against the **Swiss Ephemeris** (v2.10.03, Moshier mode, arcsecond-level — the ephemeris family Drik Panchang uses). Generated by `scripts/ephemeris-audit.mjs` (see its header for method); package `panchanga@0.2.0`.

**Scope:** years 2024–2032; locations new-delhi, calgary, london, sydney.

## Findings

1. **Ephemeris precision is NOT the dominant error source for tithi-based dates.** The engine's tithi boundaries sit a *systematic* 1.1 s from the Swiss Ephemeris (never more than 9.8 s) — far below astronomy-engine's stated ±1′ worst case (~2 min of tithi time). Across 6693 modeled festival decisions, only 0 tithi/moonrise decision(s) flipped.

2. **The sidereal solar side is CALIBRATED to Swiss-Lahiri.** Saṅkrānti ingress instants agree within 36.7 s (ayanāṁśa offset -0.00″ mean) — the O4 recalibration (Lahiri anchor 23.8570923° + IAU 2006 precession, pinned by Drik's 2031 London Makar Saṅkrānti = Jan 15) removed the former ~14″ / ~5.6-min model offset. 0 saṅkrānti-family flip(s) remain in this sweep.

3. **Sunrise & kāla windows are a non-issue** (≤ 3.5 s offset) — window edges contribute nothing at decision level.

4. **Practical consequence:** keeping astronomy-engine is defensible (the flip rate is ~0.00% per rule-instance over this sweep, all near-boundary cases). Residual flips are razor-edge lunar decisions genuinely undecidable at the ephemeris level; they need the authority of record, not a better ephemeris.

## Caveats

- Swiss Ephemeris runs in **Moshier** mode (no data files): arcsecond-level, not the
  DE431-file mode; residuals vs JPL are ≲1″ for the Moon — negligible here.
- Kāla windows and sunrises stay engine-computed on both sides of the replay; their
  engine-vs-Swiss offset is measured separately above and is orders of magnitude below
  the tithi-boundary offsets.
- "Unmodeled" rule-instances (self-check failed, or rite-specific moonrise fallback) are
  excluded from flip claims; their count is visible in the table above.
- The `margin` column measures the smallest tithi-boundary-to-window-edge gap. A flip with a
  LARGE margin is a **fraction-tie flip**: two candidate days' window-coverage fractions sit
  within seconds of equality under `max-window-fraction`, so the winner swaps on a sub-minute
  boundary shift. Such dates are genuinely undecidable at the ephemeris level and need the
  authority of record.
